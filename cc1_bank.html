1) Candy Factory Production

Source: CC1 Test-out Assessment Blueprint – 6.RP.3b

Standard: 6.RP.3b Solve unit rate problems including those involving unit pricing and constant speed.

DOK: 2

Weight: 3

When Jack began working at the candy factory, he could only pack 75 boxes of 20 candies each hour. Now, after working there six months, Jack packs 180 boxes of 20 candies each hour. How many more candies is Jack packing now during an eight-hour shift than when he started six months ago?

1 pt. for the correct answer
1 pt. for showing work that could lead to the correct answer
1 pt. for an explanation that demonstrates understanding of the problem
Answer: 16,800 more candies
Rationale:

Initial rate per hour: \[ 75 \times 20 = 1500 \text{ candies} \] New rate per hour: \[ 180 \times 20 = 3600 \text{ candies} \] Difference per hour: \[ 3600 - 1500 = 2100 \] Over 8 hours: \[ 2100 \times 8 = 16,800 \]

3) Motorcycle Gas

Source: CC1 Test-out Assessment

Standard: 6.RP.3b Use ratio and rate reasoning to solve real-world problems.

DOK: 2

Weight: 2

It took \( \frac{1}{4} \) of a gallon of gas to drive my motorcycle \( 3\frac{1}{2} \) miles. How much gas will it take to drive 1 mile?

1 pt. for the correct answer
1 pt. for showing work that could lead to the correct answer
Answer: \( \frac{1}{14} \) gallon
Rationale:

Convert mixed number: \[ 3\frac{1}{2} = \frac{7}{2} \] Unit rate: \[ \frac{1}{4} \div \frac{7}{2} \] Multiply by reciprocal: \[ \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{2}{7} = \frac{2}{28} = \frac{1}{14} \] It takes \( \frac{1}{14} \) gallon per mile.

18) True or False – Powers

Source: CC1 Test-out Assessment

DOK: 1

Answer: True
Rationale:
